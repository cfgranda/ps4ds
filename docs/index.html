Probability and Statistics for Data Science

This website contains the materials for the book Probability and Statistics for Data Science, published by Cambridge University Press:

  • A free preprint
  • 102 Python notebooks using 23 real-world datasets
  • 115 videos with slides
  • Solutions to 200 exercises

The book is a self-contained guide to the two pillars of data science, probability theory, and statistics, which are presented side by side, in order to illuminate the connections between them. The topics include random variables, nonparametric and parametric models, correlation, estimation of population parameters, hypothesis testing, principal component analysis, and both linear and nonlinear methods for regression and classification. Throughout the book, real-world datasets are used to demonstrate concepts in practice and confront readers with fundamental challenges in data science , such as overfitting, the curse of dimensionality, and causal inference.

Author: Carlos Fernandez-Granda is an Associate Professor of Mathematics and Data Science at the Courant Institute and the Center for Data Science in New York University, where he has spent the past 10 years teaching probability and statistics to data-science students. His research focuses on applications of artificial intelligence and machine learning to medicine, imaging, climate and other scientific domains.
